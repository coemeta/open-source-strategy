</summary>

CoÎµmeta is an attempt at an [infinite game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finite_and_Infinite_Games). _The goal of infinite games is not to win, but to keep playing._

We call this the **_metagame_** (as opposed to endgame):

### _Notes on mission & vision statements_
</summary>

[Mission & Vision statements](https://www.bain.com/insights/management-tools-mission-and-vision-statements/) are notoriously wonky & vacuous standard MBA-ware. But they can be useful exercises in distillation of values & intention, & sometimes produce helpful mantras for orientation & alignment. Sort of qualitative "north stars".
 
The rule of thumb we use: **Vision** describes the __big picture outcome__ you want to realize, **Mission** describes _**how**_ you'll do it.
 
It's hard to be simultaneously concise, substantive & clear, so lapsing into banal tropes & cliches is a common pitfall â€” which we haven't yet managed to entirely avoid. Familiar shorthands are useful compressions! But also risk total [semantic satiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantic_satiation) & meaninglessness. So we haven't yet found a satisfying alternative to the facile "[better world / place](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B8C5sjjhsso) / [communities](https://beta-share.descript.com/view/BBwMr30NgNy?t=473)" shibboleths.

As mentioned, we're not fully satisfied with these, due to cliches, clunky phrasing & some niche jargon (sensemaking, positive externalities, etc). But the general vibe is right, & we try to clarify the jargon via context, elaboration & reference links. We also include verbs in the vision as well as nouns, to emphasize the importance of __process__ beyond any static outcome or state. Will continue to workshop, like everything else.

#### 2. Revised [Vision](#vision)
</summary>

The true original version of this was:

  >  _A **better world** through **better information**_

But soon jettisoned the ["better world" trope](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B8C5sjjhsso) in favor of _a bit_ more concreteness, specificity & localism, with various permutations of:

  > _Better **places**, **process** & **relation** through **better information**_

 By 2026, this again felt like a bit too much & yet not enough: longwinded, yet narrowly focused on "information" as _the work_.

 It was missing essential components of regeneration & sociality. ("Relations" are mentioned, but positioned as more of a side-effect of information work, rather than an area of effort in their own right). 

I wrestled with this a bit: data & sensemaking (infowork) _was_ the main offering of CoÎµmeta as previously conceived (& my primary area of expertise & professional experience). 

But it is decidely not the totality of _work to be done_, or that I feel increasingly called to do. And _"more integrated"_ was always right there in the [metagame](#the-metagame-see-notes-for-details), as it still is. And I was certainly living this more integrated vision, so might as well say it. 

So I tentatively landed on: 

> _**Systemic regeneration**Â through **better information** & **collaboration**_

_...or more simply:_

> _Making **better sense** for a **better world**, **together**_

It occured to me that all the "better places, process...etc" can really fall under the unbrella of regenerative work, but also wanted to stress the aspect of multiple domains or systems in need of regeneration. (this is, afterall, the [polycrisis](https://news.lifeitself.org/p/from-polycrisis-to-metacrisis-a-short) / [metacrisis](https://perspecteeva.substack.com/p/prefixing-the-world))

* Multi-system regenerationÂ ...

While "multi-system..." or simply "systems.." were probably the most correct formulations, I felt like "systemic regeneration" was the most likely to be understood by the most people at first blush. "Systemic change" is a familiar enough trope, so this seemed like the _least-arcane-jargon_ option. (Though I may change my mind). 

Either way, "systemic regeneration" is still a bit of a mouthful, & its meaning likely not immediately obvious to most people. But I'm generally ok with this because:

1. I also brought back the generic "better world" trope as a simpler alternative for this reason
2. Vision statements are meant to be a bit evocative, while the paired Mission statement is meant to be more concrete.
